About Me

I am a graduate student and Prime Minister's Research Fellow in the Department of Computer Science at Indian Institute of Technology, Delhi. My primary research interest is in Computer Vision and Deep Learning. I am particularly interested in developing accurate, explainable, and geenralizable Vision systems for healthcare related tasks such as medical image analysis.

Prior to joining the Ph.D. program at IIT Delhi, I have worked as a Member of Technical Staff at Adobe systems (Bangalore, India) and as a Research Assistant at The Pennsylvania State University (State College, USA). I completed my Masters from IIT Delhi in 2015 and my Bachelors from IIEST Shibpur in 2012. I achieved All India Rank 99 out of 224160 candidates (99.96 percentile) in GATE 2013 (Computer Science).

Research

My research in IIT Delhi specifically aims to tackle the problem of Gallbladder Cancer (GBC) detection from abdominal ultrasound (USG) images. Through my research, I expect to develop the automated processing of USG images in general and understand the objective characterization of gallbladder malignancy in USG images. Gallbladder cancer is an important problem for Indian population. In the Northern parts of India, the incidence rate is comparable to the global highest. GBC causes about 165,000 deaths every year, worldwide. The disease is often detected late and has an average survival duration of only six months. AI-assisted detection of gallbladder malignancy signs in USG would result in early detection and ameliorate the bleak survival scenario. Following are some of my works.
