Research Projects

  • Randomized Rounding using Random Walks (Jun,2014 — Jun,2015):
    Rounding algorithms are particularly of interest since they are often used to find near-optimal approximate solutions of integer programs in polynomial time. Even though the conventional method (proposed by Raghvan and Thompson) of rounding initial feasible solution of a corresponding relaxed linear program has tight error bound in the worst case, we went beyond that and investigated techniques that could produce better bounds for the ‘average-case’ inputs. Our method used an iterative transformation of initial solution using multidimensional random walk followed by constructive Lovász Local Lemma and produced a much better error bound for average case inputs.
    Full Thesis | PPT
  • Design of Test Simulator for Microfluidic Biochips (Jun,2011 — May,2012):
    Microuidic biochips offer promising platform for parallel DNA analysis, automated drug discovery etc. It is important to ensure high reliability of biochip funcitonality for safety- critical applications. We proposed a new technique for functional testing of bio chips for customized droplet route-paths. The average completion time for the customized testing method was nearly 10-times faster than that of Parallel Scan and Peripheral Scan testing methods for In-Vitro test benches. The average number of cells traversed in customized testing was nearly 6-10% of the Parallel and Peripheral scanning methods.

  • SIC/XE Assembler:
    Simplified Instructional Computer(SIC) is a hypothetical architecture that was used by Leland Beck in his book 'System Software' to explain the concepts of assemblers, compilers and operating systems. SIC/XE,(the XE stands for Extra Equipment), is an extension of SIC which has higher memory, greater number of registers and additional instructions. The task of the assembler was to convert the nmemonics and symbolic operands to their machine codes and finally write the object program in HEX code.
